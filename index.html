The Colosseum

The Roman Colosseum


The Roman Colosseum is a structre that was made from 72 AD to 80 AD. It was made with the purpose of being a place that hosted
gladiator fights, open executions, dramas, mock battles, and re-enactments of famous battles. The former roman emperor Vespasian
made the Colosseum as part of an imperial effort to revitalize Rome. The Colosseum can be visted today in present day Rome, Italy

The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall Of China


The Great Wall of China is a series of fortafications that rung along the historical northern borders of
ancient Chinese states and Imperial China as protection against various nomadic groups from the Eurasian Steppe.
Apart from defense, other purposes of the Great Wall have included border controls, allowing the imposition of duties
on goods transported along the Silk Road, regulation or encouragement of trade and the control of immigration and emigration.
Today it can be view as a popular tourist spot in china.

The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal


The Taj Mahal is an Islamic ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city
of Agra. It was commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favourite wife,
Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself. The Taj Mahal today can be visted by tourist from 30 Minutes
before Sunrise up to 30 Minutes before Sunset.

Christ the Redeemer

The Christ Redeemer


The Christ Redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, created by French sculptor
Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot.
Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida sculpted the face. Constructed between 1922 and 1931, the statue is 30 metres high,
excluding its 8-metre pedestal. The arms stretch 28 metres wide. It is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone.
Today tourist are able to view and vist the Chirst Redeemer today.


Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu


Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru on a 2,430-meter mountain range.
It is located in the Machupicchu District within Urubamba Province[4] above the Sacred Valley, which is 80 kilometers northwest
of Cusco. The Urubamba River flows past it, cutting through the Cordillera and creating a canyon with a tropical mountain climate.
Tourist are able to book tours and such to tour and view Machu Picchu.


Chichén Itzá

Chichén Itzá


Chichen Itza was a major focal point in the Northern Maya Lowlands from the Late Classic from 600 AD to 900 AD through the
Terminal Classic from 800 AD to 900 AD and into the early portion of the Postclassic period wich was from 900 AD to 1200 AD.
The site exhibits a multitude of architectural styles, reminiscent of styles seen in central Mexico and of the Puuc and Chenes
styles of the Northern Maya lowlands. The presence of central Mexican styles was once thought to have been representative of
direct migration or even conquest from central Mexico, but most contemporary interpretations view the presence of these non-Maya
styles more as the result of cultural diffusion. Tourist are able to view Chichén Itzá today and its considered to be one of mexicos
most toursits attraction


Petra

Petra


Petra, originally known to its inhabitants as Raqmu or Raqēmō, is an historic and archaeological city in southern Jordan.
It is adjacent to the mountain of Jabal Al-Madbah, in a basin surrounded by mountains forming the eastern flank of the
Arabah valley running from the Dead Sea to the Gulf of Aqaba.The area around Petra has been inhabited from as early as
7000 BC, and the Nabataeans might have settled in what would become the capital city of their kingdom as early as the
4th century BC. Today tourist are required to have the Jordan pass to view petra and 40 other attractions.
